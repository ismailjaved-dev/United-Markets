Risk management in trading involves strategies and techniques used to minimize potential losses and preserve capital while trading in financial markets.

Risk management is crucial for traders to protect their investments from unexpected market movements and adverse events, helping to maintain consistency and long-term profitability.

Common risk management techniques include setting stop-loss orders, diversifying portfolios, managing position sizes, utilizing leverage cautiously, and employing risk-reward ratios in trading strategies.

Determining your risk tolerance involves assessing your financial situation, investment goals, time horizon, and emotional capacity to withstand market fluctuations and potential losses.

A stop-loss order is an instruction placed with a broker to automatically sell a security when it reaches a predetermined price level, limiting the trader's loss on a position.
